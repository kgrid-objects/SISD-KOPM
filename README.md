**Specify–Implement–Serve–Deploy Knowledge Object Publication Model**

The **SISD-KOPM** is a publication model for organizing, preserving, and evolving **published computational capabilities**. Its purpose is to enable evidence-based computational capabilities to become durable scholarly and operational objects that can be discovered, evaluated, referenced, corroborated, realized, deployed, and repeatedly applied at scale.

Unlike traditional software packaging approaches, the SISD-KOPM treats computational capabilities as a new class of publication. A published computational capability possesses a persistent identity, accumulates provenance and corroborating evidence over time, evolves through versioned releases, and supports reproducibility from its foundational specification.

The SISD-KOPM organizes each published computational capability into four realization layers:

- **Specify** — Computable Knowledge Specification (CKS)
- **Implement** — Implementation Representations (IRs)
- **Serve** — Service Realizations (SRs)
- **Deploy** — Deployment Packages (DPs)

Together these layers define a coherent publication unit containing everything required to identify, understand, corroborate, realize, invoke, and prepare a computational capability for operational application. Actual deployment, execution, and operational use intentionally remain outside the publication boundary.

## Purpose

The long-term goal of the SISD-KOPM is to make evidence-based computational capabilities preferred scholarly and operational resources that people, software systems, and AI agents can repeatedly discover, reference, evaluate, deploy, and apply.

By organizing computational capabilities around durable full-fledged scientific publications rather than routine operational ecosystem specific software artifacts, the SISD-KOPM seeks to support:

- reproducibility of computational capabilities
- persistent scholarly identity
- canonical executable realizations
- accumulation of corroborating evidence
- long-term stewardship and versioned evolution
- broad operational applicability across computing environments

## Design Principles

The published computable capability architecture is founded on several core principles, including:

- Publishing of computational capabilities over computable knowledge
- Publication model inclusive of packaging
- Incremental realization
- Referenceability
- Corroboration
- Extended operational reach
- Reproducibility from specification

## Project Status

The SISD-KOPM is an active research and development effort. The architecture, specifications, and supporting materials continue to evolve while preserving architectural continuity across versions.
